About

The following maps were done as part of the DataKind DataDive held at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, August 27th, 2016

Wheat is an important crop in Ethiopia. Favorable environments for wheat ar also favorable for wheat rust, which is a fungal disease that can devastate crops. In 2010 Ethiopia experienced the largest yellow rust epidemic in recent history with over 600,000 ha of wheat affected (losses in individual fields around 70-80%) and an estimated US 3.2 million spent on fungicides. The stem rust epidemics in 2013, 2014 and also some in 2015 were estimated to cover around 30-50,000 ha and were more localized in the southern areas. Losses here were 100% in the worst affected areas.

Dave Hodson of CIMMYT coordinates BGRI (Borlaug Global Rust Initiative). They have been collecting the survey data shown here.

Each point describes one sample in these surveys. They are colored according to the severity of Stem Rust identified in this location.

Click on a point to see data from remote sensing of reflectance in this location.
